Martian terrain

The surface of Mars is lifeless and dusty with reddish soil formed from volcanic rocks. Mars has many canyons, volcanoes, river beds, and craters. Swamps are also added to these terrain types after terraforming began. The reddish color of Mars' terrain is caused by the chemical break-down of iron-rich rocks.

Following types of terrain exist on Red planet:
  • Wasteland
  • Desert
  • Plains
  • Tundra
  • Crater
  • Ice
  • Chasm
  • Misty mountains
  • Hills
  • Mountains
  • Swamp

Transformation

Engineers can be used to transform Martian terrain increasing food production but removing any bonus resources and improvements in the process.

Transformation is a difficult process and will take some time before completion.
